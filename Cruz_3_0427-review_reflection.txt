The essence of human / higher level learning is developing the skill to discriminate patterns and 
identify shared characteristics across events (stimuli).
Memorization - the regurgitation of facts - is considered the lowest level of learning.
Why, then, are students encouraged to learn CS?
It can help them learn to trouble-shoot. If something is wrong they can try to figure out what it 
is.

What should students learn from CS?
How to get better at trouble-shooting.
